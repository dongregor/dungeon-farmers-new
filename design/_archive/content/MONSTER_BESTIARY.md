## Design Notes

### Capture Rate Philosophy
- Common: 10-15% (easy to get, fill basic slots)
- Uncommon: 8-10% (slightly harder, better stats)
- Rare: 5-8% (requires farming, worthwhile)
- Epic: 3-5% (challenging, powerful)
- Legendary: 1-3% (rare drops, chase monsters)
- Mythic: 0.5-1% (end-game completionist)

### Stat Scaling
Base stats scale with rarity:
- Common: 80-90% of expected power
- Uncommon: 90-100%
- Rare: 100-110%
- Epic: 110-120%
- Legendary: 120-135%
- Mythic: 135-150%

### Parody Balance
- 70% Gaming/RPG references (slimes, shiny Pokemon, tutorial enemies)
- 20% Corporate satire (managers, consultants, burnout)
- 10% Meta humor (self-awareness, game mechanics)

### Loot Table Philosophy
- High % drops: Common materials (used frequently)
- Mid % drops: Uncommon materials (crafting)
- Low % drops: Rare materials (epic equipment)
- Special drops: Thematic items (buzzwords, coffee, stress)

## Design Philosophy Summary

### Parody Balance Achieved
- **Gaming/RPG Parody**: 70% (slimes, grinding, RNG, shiny variants, tutorial enemies)
- **Corporate Satire**: 20% (managers, consultants, office politics, burnout)
- **Meta Humor**: 10% (self-awareness, fourth wall breaks, game mechanics)

### Monster Design Principles
1. **Every monster has personality** - Not just stats, but character
2. **Self-aware humor** - Monsters know they're in a game
3. **Balanced distribution** - Variety across rarities and archetypes
4. **Capture-worthy** - Players should want to collect them all
5. **Dungeon-ready** - Each monster has clear dungeon role

### Loot Table Philosophy
- **Common materials**: 70-80% drop rate (frequent crafting)
- **Uncommon materials**: 40-60% drop rate (moderate use)
- **Rare materials**: 20-40% drop rate (epic crafting)
- **Special drops**: 5-15% drop rate (unique items)
