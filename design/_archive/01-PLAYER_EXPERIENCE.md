## Phase 0: First Login (0-5 minutes)

### The Hook - Clean Welcome

Player arrives at clean, professional landing screen with minimal friction:
- **[Create New Guild]** button (no account barriers)
- **Tone established immediately**: Modern, readable UI with playful RPG parody (corporate flavor is *optional seasoning* in system/UI copy)
- **Promise visible**: "Build your weird little adventuring guild (and make it efficient if you want)"

### Character Creation - FAST & NO REGRETS

**Screen 1: Guild Name**
- Simple text input with suggested names
- Optional tabard color selection (cosmetic only)
- **NO excessive customization** (respects time principle)

**Screen 2: Choose Your First Hero**
- **Only 3 hero options** (prevents analysis paralysis)
- **All three equally viable** (no trap choices)
- Clear visual and mechanical differences

**Hero Choices:**
1. **THERON** (Warrior) - Balanced stats, HP/ATK focus, Tags: Stalwart + Leader
2. **LYRA** (Ranger) - High ATK/SPD, low DEF, Tags: Swift + Precise
3. **MAGNUS** (Mage) - Magic damage, defensive, Tags: Arcane + Wise

**Key Design Decision**: Stats shown as simple visual indicators (stars), tags introduced immediately with tooltips. Player knows they can recruit others later (reduces FOMO).

**‚ùì [QUESTION]**: Should guild creation include guild master character customization (appearance, name) or focus purely on first hero selection?

## Key Design Principles Applied

### From Player Research Findings

‚úÖ **No energy systems** - Play as much or as little as you want
‚úÖ **No VIP tiers** - QoL features available to everyone (no pay-for-convenience)
‚úÖ **Meaningful progression** - Always moving forward, no failure states
‚úÖ **Respect player time** - Quick 5-min check-ins OR deep 2-hour sessions both valid
‚úÖ **Fair monetization** - Cosmetics only, no pay-to-win mechanics
‚úÖ **Social without pressure** - Alliance benefits available, but solo play totally viable
‚úÖ **Content creation loop** - Personal dungeons give players agency and creativity
‚úÖ **Clear goals** - Collections, equipment sets, visible progression paths
‚úÖ **Dopamine hits** - Regular rewards at all engagement levels (casual to hardcore)

### Avoided Pitfalls (Anti-Patterns)

‚ùå **No arbitrary time gates** - Expeditions have real durations, but no "wait 24 hours to continue"
‚ùå **No required duplicates** - All heroes viable without dupes (no gacha trap)
‚ùå **No FOMO pressure** - Events return annually, cosmetics don't affect power
‚ùå **No pay-only content** - Everything accessible through gameplay
‚ùå **No forced PvP** - Competitive leaderboards optional, cooperation encouraged
‚ùå **No punishing failure states** - Efficiency variance (60-100%+) instead of complete failure
‚ùå **No daily grind burnout** - Quick sessions valid, offline progress meaningful

## Why This Journey Works

### Gradual Complexity Introduction
Systems introduced one at a time:
1. Expeditions (first 5 min)
2. Equipment (after first completion)
3. Monster capture (second expedition)
4. Multi-hero teams (30 min in)
5. Personal dungeons (2-5 hours in)
6. Alliances (5-10 hours in)
7. Ascension (20+ hours in)

**Player never feels overwhelmed.**

### Constant Rewards
Something new every session:
- Immediate: Expedition loot, equipment drops
- Short-term: Daily quests, dungeon farms
- Medium-term: Set completion, monster captures
- Long-term: Collection milestones, ascension

**Player always has a reason to log in.**

### Clear Milestones
Visible progress markers:
- Player level (1-50+)
- Power rating (visible number)
- Collection percentages (45/100 heroes)
- Set bonuses (2/4, 4/4 complete)
- Alliance contributions (raid participation)

**Player sees tangible growth.**

### Strategic Depth
Multiple viable paths:
- Focus on zones for monster captures
- Focus on missions for guaranteed rewards
- Focus on personal dungeons for efficient farming
- Focus on alliances for social benefits
- Focus on collections for completion rewards

**No single "correct" strategy.**

### Social Integration
Alliance content feels natural:
- Passive bonuses (always active)
- Optional raids (3-5 players, not entire alliance)
- Real-time chat (build friendships)
- Shared goals (alliance level progression)

**Social play enhances but doesn't gatekeep.**

### Player Agency
Personal dungeons = creative expression:
- Choose which monsters to assign
- Optimize loot tables
- Balance durability vs power
- Experiment with schematic combinations

**Players build their own content.**

### Long-Term Goals
Collection completion drives engagement:
- 100 unique heroes to recruit
- 150 monster species to capture
- 50 dungeon schematics to collect
- 30 equipment sets to complete

**Months of meaningful progression.**

### Respect for Time
Offline progress matters:
- 8-hour expeditions while sleeping
- Alliance bonuses always active
- Personal dungeons don't expire (durability-based)
- No "check in every 4 hours or lose progress"

**Players play on THEIR schedule.**

---

**The journey ensures players learn systems without overwhelm, always have meaningful choices, and experience the unique monster capture + personal dungeon loop that differentiates Dungeon Farmers from the saturated idle RPG market.**
