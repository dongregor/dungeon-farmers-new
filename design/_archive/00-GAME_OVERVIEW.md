# Dungeon Farmers - Game Overview

**Genre**: Idle RPG with Strategic Depth
**Platform**: Web (browser-based), expandable to PWA, Steam, Mobile
**Target Audience**: Exhausted gacha/mobile players seeking fair, strategic gameplay
**Core Hook**: Monster Capture + Personal Dungeon Building

---

## üéØ Elevator Pitch

> **"The idle RPG that respects your time and wallet."**

Dungeon Farmers is a web-based idle RPG where you manage a guild of heroes, capture monsters during expeditions, and build personal dungeons for optimized resource farming. No energy systems. No predatory gacha. No pay-to-win. Just strategic team building, creative dungeon design, and meaningful progression.

**Think**: Fantasy RPG parody that lovingly mocks gacha mechanics, MMO grinds, and LitRPG tropes while delivering **players create their own farming dungeons**.

### 1. Respect Player Time

- **Free demo ‚Üí $9.99 unlock** (play 10-15 hours free, pay once for full game)
- **Cosmetics only** for premium purchases
- **No energy systems** - Play as much as you want

- **No pay-to-win** - All heroes obtainable through gameplay

### 3. Player Agency & Creativity
- **Personal dungeon creation** (UNIQUE HOOK - see below)
- **Multiple viable team compositions**
- **Strategic optimization** over mindless grinding
- **Player-generated content** that creates self-sustaining loops
- **Meaningful choices** in team building, equipment, dungeon design

## üìñ Terminology & Glossary

### Core Game Terms

**Personal Dungeons**: Player-created dungeon instances built using Schematics and captured Monsters. These are the game's unique differentiator, allowing players to create optimized farming content.

**Efficiency Variance**: The 60-150% performance outcome range for expeditions and dungeon runs. Determines reward quantity based on team composition, equipment, and strategic choices. (Parody of gacha "SSS Rank" systems, but without the frustration)

**Schematic**: Blueprint for building a personal dungeon. Defines monster slot requirements (e.g., "3x Melee + 2x Caster"), dungeon layout, and reward types. Collected through expeditions, raids, and story missions. (Parodies "crafting recipe" hoarding in MMOs)

**Alliance Points**: Currency earned through alliance activities (raids, donations, participation). Used in alliance shop for exclusive rewards. (Mocks endless "guild tokens" from MMOs)

**Capture Rate**: Probability of capturing a monster during expeditions. Base rate varies by monster rarity and expedition difficulty (5-15% typical range). Affected by hero traits and equipment bonuses. (Gentle parody of "0.6% SSR rates" - but actually fair)

**Durability**: Lifespan of a personal dungeon measured in runs (10-20 depending on schematic rarity). Once durability reaches zero, the dungeon must be rebuilt with new monster assignments. (References "weapon durability" from Zelda/Dark Souls, applied to dungeons)

**Set Bonus**: Equipment effect activated when wearing multiple pieces from the same set. Provides stat bonuses and special effects at 2-piece, 4-piece, and 6-piece thresholds. (Classic MMO/Diablo mechanic, no parody needed - it's fun)

**Trait**: Passive ability or characteristic that defines hero personality and provides gameplay effects. Each hero has 3-5 traits with rarities from Common to Legendary. Examples: "Main Character Energy", "NPC Awareness", "Tutorial Skipper".

**Personality Dimensions** (Phase 2+): Five core personality axes (0-10 scale) that determine hero behavior and event probabilities: Sociability (Loner ‚Üî Social Butterfly), Work Ethic (Relaxed ‚Üî Workaholic), Risk Tolerance (Cautious ‚Üî Thrill-Seeker), Competitiveness (Team Player ‚Üî Solo Glory), Seriousness (Goofy ‚Üî Professional). Affects story event generation and team dynamics.

**Morale/Happiness** (Phase 2+): Hero mood state (0-100 scale) ranging from Burnout (0-14, refuses missions) to Inspired (85-100, +15% stats). Affected by mission outcomes, equipment upgrades, personality preferences, and management decisions. Creates dynamic "employee wellness" gameplay with corporate satire framing ("HR alerts", "mandatory PTO", "performance reviews").

**Tag**: Hero ability category used for expedition requirements and team synergies. Examples: Fire Magic, Healing, Stealth, AoE Damage. Heroes have 1-3 tags that define their strategic role. (RPG classic, works as-is)

**Familiarity**: Bonus system for repeated zone expeditions. Heroes gain +1% damage per clear (max +50%), encouraging specialization while maintaining flexibility. (Parodies "farming the same dungeon 1000 times" MMO trope - but makes it beneficial)

**Retirement/Ascension** (In-game: "Early Retirement Program"): Prestige system where level 50 heroes reset to level 1 with enhanced base stats and exclusive bonuses.

**Personal Dungeon** (In-game: "Personal Resource Development Initiative" or "Revenue Stream #X"): Player-created farming content with "quarterly earnings reports" (resource generation tracking).

### System-Specific Terms

**Monster Families**: Categorization of monsters by type (Beast, Undead, Elemental, Humanoid, Dragon, Demon, Construct). Used for dungeon synergy bonuses when assigning monsters of the same family to schematic slots.

**Synergy Bonus**: Reward multiplier (5-25%) gained when personal dungeons use monsters from the same family or meet thematic requirements.

**Affix** (Phase 4): Prefix/suffix modifiers on Rare+ equipment providing additional random stats. Examples: "Blazing" (+Fire Damage), "Sturdy" (+Defense).

**Enchant Slot** (Phase 4): Socket on Legendary+ equipment for customizable bonuses. Players can insert enchants for specific stat boosts or effects.

**Mythic Variant** (Phase 4): Rare procedurally-generated monster subtype with unique visual variants and enhanced power (110-130% base stats). Example: "Frost-Touched Orc" instead of standard "Orc Warrior".

**Gear Score**: Numerical representation of equipment power (1-1000+). Contributes ~30-40% of total hero power, with the remainder from base stats, traits, and set bonuses.

**Ascension Path** (Phase 3+): Specialization choice during prestige. Options include Offensive (damage-focused), Defensive (survivability), or Balanced (hybrid approach).

**Alliance Raid Tier**: Difficulty classification for cooperative content. Tiers: Beginner (3-5 players, lower requirements), Intermediate (coordination required), Advanced (optimized teams), Elite (endgame challenge).

**Slot Requirement**: Schematic constraint defining monster types needed to build a personal dungeon. Example: "2x Tank + 1x Healer + 2x DPS". Monsters must match archetype or family to fill slots.

## ‚ú® Unique Selling Proposition

### The Hook: Monster Capture + Personal Dungeon Building

**What makes Dungeon Farmers different from every other idle RPG?**

**The Self-Sustaining Content Loop:**

1. **Capture Monsters**: During expeditions (zones, missions), heroes have a 5-15% chance to capture monsters
2. **Collect Schematics**: Find dungeon blueprints that define monster slot requirements (e.g., "3x Melee + 2x Caster")
3. **Build Personal Dungeons**: Assign captured monsters to schematic slots to create functioning dungeons
4. **Farm Optimized Resources**: Run personal dungeons (durability-based, 10-20 runs) for targeted resource farming
5. **Progress Heroes**: Use resources to level/equip heroes for harder content
6. **Capture Rarer Monsters**: Tackle harder expeditions to get better monsters
7. **Build Better Dungeons**: Create more optimized dungeons with rare monsters

**Why This Matters:**
- ‚úÖ Solves **content starvation** (idle game's biggest problem)
- ‚úÖ Gives **player creativity** and ownership
- ‚úÖ Creates **strategic optimization** gameplay
- ‚úÖ Provides **long-term engagement** without developer content sprints
- ‚úÖ Differentiates from **all competitors**

## üéÆ Core Gameplay Loop

### Minute-to-Minute Loop (Session Start)
1. Check completed expeditions
2. Claim rewards (gold, XP, equipment, monsters)
3. Level up heroes, equip new gear
4. Send heroes on new expeditions (2-60 minutes)
5. (Optional) Check personal dungeons, rebuild if needed
6. Close game or dive deeper into optimization

### Hour-to-Hour Loop (Active Play)
1. Complete 5-10 expeditions
2. Recruit 1-2 new heroes (tavern system)
3. Capture 2-4 monsters
4. Optimize team compositions for specific content
5. Build/rebuild personal dungeons for better resources
6. Work toward equipment sets (2-piece, 4-piece bonuses)

### Day-to-Day Loop (Progression)
1. Unlock new zones (exploration content)
2. Complete story missions (guaranteed rewards)
3. Upgrade personal dungeons with better monsters
4. Chase equipment sets for specific builds
5. Recruit specialized heroes for content gaps
6. Join or manage alliance activities

### Week-to-Week Loop (Long-term Goals)
1. Max hero ascension (level 50 ‚Üí prestige system)
2. Complete equipment set collections
3. Build optimized dungeon portfolio (multiple specialized dungeons)
4. Participate in alliance raids (3-5 player cooperative content)
5. Chase rare monster captures for perfect dungeon builds
6. Optimize resource generation efficiency

- **6 equipment slots** with set bonuses (2/4/6-piece)
- **No gacha** - Recruit with gold, earn through story

### Monster Capture & Personal Dungeons (UNIQUE)
- **Capture monsters** during expeditions (5-15% chance)
- **Collect schematics** (dungeon blueprints with monster requirements)
- **Build dungeons** by assigning monsters to schematic slots
- **Durability-based** (10-20 runs), NOT time-limited
- **Optimized farming** - Create dungeons for specific resources
- **Monster power scaling** (80-120% of team average)

### Expedition Content
- **Zones** (exploration) - Repeatable, RNG rewards, monster captures, familiarity system
- **Missions** (story) - Guaranteed rewards, narrative content, specific monsters
- **Dungeons** (equipment farming) - Set pieces, boss mechanics
- **Raids** (alliance) - 3-5 player cooperative, premium rewards

### Alliance System
- **Max 30 members** per alliance
- **Alliance raids** (3-5 players, NOT entire alliance)
- **Shared bonuses**: +5-15% Gold, +3-10% XP, +1-5% rare drops
- **Real-time chat** (future: voice chat for raids)
- **Shared dungeon library** (future: rent schematics from allies)

### Equipment & Progression
- **6 equipment slots**: Weapon, Armor, Helmet, Boots, Accessory 1, Accessory 2
- **Set bonuses**: 2-piece (+10% stat), 4-piece (+20% stat + special effect), 6-piece (build-defining)
- **Rarity tiers**: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary, Mythic
- **Gear score** (1-1000+) determines power contribution
- **No equipment gacha** - Farm dungeons, craft from materials
